Hello there, and welcome to my homepage!

I am an Assistant Professor of Operations, Information, and Decisions at The Wharton School, University of Pennsylvania. My research focuses on the design of online marketplaces using data, stochastic models, and game theory. I am passionate about solving problems to help improve access to EVs for low-income individuals and sharing assets in emerging assets, incentivizing consumers to reduce peak grid loads. I am also interested in understanding how LLMs are impacting knowledge-sharing networks. My research has been recognized and selected as a finalist for the IBM Best Student Paper, the IBM Service Science Best Cluster Paper, and I won the best student presentation in INFORMS. Previously, I completed my PhD in Operations Research at The Kellogg School, Northwestern University in 2023. I also teach undergraduate elective, OIDD 2200: Operations Management Analytics in Spring.

I maintain a list of my publications, research projects, and implementations under the Research tab, and a list of other non-research projects under the Projects tab. You may also check out my Github profile.

To get a better insight on my life so far, you can have a look at my curriculum vitae. I blog (although quite less frequently) and pin them down here. Feel free to read a bit more about me.
